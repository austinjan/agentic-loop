Quarterly Sales Report — Q4 2025
==================================

Region: Asia Pacific
Prepared by: Operations Team
Date: 2025-12-31

Executive Summary
------------------
This quarter showed strong growth across all product lines, with total revenue
reaching $4.2 million, a 23% increase over Q3. The launch of the new analytics
platform in October drove significant adoption in the enterprise segment.

Revenue Breakdown by Product
-----------------------------
1. Core Platform       $1,800,000  (43% of total)
2. Analytics Add-on    $1,200,000  (29% of total)
3. API Access          $  720,000  (17% of total)
4. Support Contracts   $  480,000  (11% of total)

Monthly Trend
--------------
October:    $1,150,000
November:   $1,380,000
December:   $1,670,000

Key Metrics
------------
- New customers acquired: 147
- Customer churn rate: 2.3% (down from 3.1% in Q3)
- Average deal size: $28,571
- Enterprise deals (>$100K): 12
- Net Promoter Score: 72

Regional Performance
---------------------

Korea:      $  890,000  (54 customers)

Customer Feedback Highlights
------------------------------
- "The new analytics dashboard saved our team 10 hours per week."
- "API response times improved dramatically after the October update."

- "Would love to see more customization options in the reporting module."

Challenges and Risks
----------------------
1. Increased competition from local vendors in the Korea market
2. Currency fluctuation impacting Japan revenue (¥ weakened 4% vs USD)
3. Two enterprise prospects delayed decisions to Q1 2026
4. Hiring pipeline for technical support roles remains tight

Q1 2026 Outlook
-----------------
- Projected revenue: $4.8M - $5.2M
- Planned product launches: Mobile SDK, Workflow Automation
- Target new customers: 160+
- Expansion into Vietnam and Thailand markets planned
- Annual customer conference scheduled for March 2026

Notes
------
All figures are in USD. Revenue is recognized on an accrual basis.
Customer count includes only active paying accounts as of quarter end.
Churn rate is calculated as the percentage of customers lost divided by
total customers at the start of the quarter.
